Opera Cake

Recipe Description

Opera cake is a classic French dessert made with thin layers of Joconde sponge cake, coffee syrup, coffee buttercream, chocolate ganache, and chocolate icing.

This recipe is inspired by the traditional Opera cake, a refined dessert known for its neat layers and balanced coffee and chocolate flavors.

I like this recipe because it feels elegant and professional while still being built from simple pastry components.

It is a rich and polished dessert, making it a great choice for celebrations, dinner parties, or a more advanced baking project.

Recipe Ingredients

All ingredients for this recipe are listed below:

For the cake components

  • 2 Joconde biscuit sheets, 33.5cm x 33.5cm
  • 600g coffee-flavored buttercream
  • 400g chocolate ganache

For coating the biscuit

  • 200g dark chocolate couverture

For the coffee syrup

  • 200ml sugar syrup
  • Coffee extract to taste
  • Grand Marnier or Cointreau to taste, optional

For the chocolate icing

  • 200g dark chocolate couverture
  • 30g grapeseed oil

For the finish

  • Edible gold leaf, optional

Instructions

For preparing the cake layers

  1. Prepare the Joconde biscuit sheets, coffee buttercream, chocolate ganache, and coffee syrup before assembling the cake.
  2. Once the Joconde biscuit sheets are cold, remove them gently from the baking mat or tray.
  3. Using a 20cm square cake ring as a guide, cut out three square pieces of Joconde biscuit.
  4. If needed, one of the biscuit layers can be made from two smaller pieces joined together.
  5. Melt 200g of dark chocolate couverture in short intervals, stirring often so the chocolate does not burn.
  6. Brush a very thin layer of melted chocolate over one biscuit square.
  7. Refrigerate the chocolate-coated biscuit for about 10 minutes, or until the chocolate has set.

For assembling the opera cake

  1. Place the chocolate-coated biscuit inside the square cake ring, with the chocolate-coated side facing down.
  2. Brush the top of the biscuit with coffee syrup.
  3. Spread a thin layer of coffee buttercream over the biscuit, about 3-4mm thick.
  4. Brush one side of the second biscuit layer with coffee syrup.
  5. Place the second biscuit layer over the buttercream, with the soaked side facing down.
  6. Brush the top side of the second biscuit layer with more coffee syrup.
  7. Spread a thin, even layer of chocolate ganache over the second biscuit layer.
  8. Brush one side of the third biscuit layer with coffee syrup.
  9. Place the third biscuit layer over the ganache, with the soaked side facing down.
  10. Brush the top of the third biscuit layer with the remaining coffee syrup.
  11. Spread another thin layer of coffee buttercream over the top and smooth the surface with a spatula.
  12. Cover the cake with plastic wrap directly touching the surface of the buttercream.
  13. Refrigerate the cake for at least 2 hours before glazing.

For the chocolate icing and finish

  1. Melt 200g of dark chocolate couverture over a bain-marie.
  2. Once the chocolate is fully melted, stir in the grapeseed oil until smooth.
  3. Remove the chilled cake from the refrigerator and gently remove the plastic wrap.
  4. Pour the chocolate icing over the cold buttercream surface.
  5. Smooth the icing quickly with a large spatula to create an even, thin layer.
  6. Let the icing set for about 10 minutes in the refrigerator.
  7. Gently remove the cake from the square ring.
  8. Dip a sharp knife in hot water, wipe it dry, and trim the edges of the cake for a clean finish.
  9. Decorate the top with a small piece of edible gold leaf, if using.
  10. Slice carefully and serve chilled. Enjoy!

Notes

  • The cake can be assembled the day before and glazed on the day it will be served.
  • For cleaner layers, avoid spreading buttercream or ganache onto the sides of the cake ring.
  • The chocolate coating on the bottom biscuit helps protect the sponge from moisture.
  • If you do not want to use alcohol, leave out the Grand Marnier or Cointreau from the coffee syrup.
  • For neat slices, warm the knife in hot water and wipe it clean between cuts.
